About us

What do we do?

Our specialty is statistical data analysis, but we do much more than that. We can be part of the whole process of data gathering, data preparation, statistical analysis and reporting or visualization of data. Also, we can coach people and give advice how to do specific analyses.

See Our services for more information.

Some examples of what we do:
  • collect data with surveys
  • map data from the world wide web
  • design experiments
  • merge messy data files into a clear data set
  • look for associations between variables
  • visualize patient data for group practices
  • coach people to do statistical analyses
  • help non-mathematical students with statistics
  • ...

Who could we help?

We think we can help a large number of people. In fact, anyone who has some (messy) data and wants to know the story behind it, or anyone who wants to know something and does not know how to obtain this information can contact us.

We can work for students, scientists, institutions, companies and group practices.

Experiences

We have most experience with biological data, but can learn more about other fields too. Most of the work is done by Ineke van Gremberghe (diploma’s: Master in Biotechnology, Master in Statistical Data Analysis, PhD in Biology).
 

Our services

Data Gathering

When you want to know something but don’t have any data yet, we can help to collect this. We can for instance design an experiment, gather data from surveys or collect data from the world wide web.

Data Preparation

When you have very ‘messy’ data (e.g. different excel files with different formats) we can create a clear data set for you. Data preparation involves data manipulation, data combining and data cleansing. The data manipulation involves performing various calculations and transformations to get the data in the desired format. Data combining is the process of integrating all the information. The data cleansing process is the whole of all measures to improve data integrity and quality that are taken in the 3 first steps.

Statistical Analysis

An important part of our work is statistical data analysis. We work normally with the freely available software R, but can also work with SAS or SPSS. We can do descriptive analyses, regression analyses (e.g. general, generalized, random effects, longitudinal data), multivariate analyses, and causal mediation analyses. Other statistical methods can be discussed.

Data Visualization

The data is visualized by clear figures, which is part of the descriptive analyses. The statistical analyses performed and the results obtained are explained in a detailed report if preferred.

Teaching, coaching and advice

Statistics is not an easy course for some students with few mathematical background. We can help students who follow non-mathematical studies by teaching statistics or helping with statistical analysis. Some people want practical advice on how to do a specific analysis in R, SAS or SPSS. We can coach and give advice on the analyses described before in 'Statistical Analysis'.
